Dragon tiger phoenix

Dragon and Phoenix is a traditional Chinese dish that incorporates two symbolic animals in Chinese mythology. The dish is made by stir-frying succulent chicken and shrimp with vegetables such as mushrooms, water chestnuts, and bamboo shoots seasoned with Chinese spices, soy sauce, and oyster sauce. The presentation of the dish sees the chicken and shrimp arranged on each side of the plate, resembling the two mythical creatures, often served during auspicious occasions such as weddings to represent balance and harmony.

  • Rating :

  • Time : 30 Mins

  • Portions : 4-6 servings

  • Tags : Dinner, Main, Chicken, Dragon, Phoenix

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast, sliced into thin strips
  • 1/2 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions

1In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and rice vinegar. Set aside.

2In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over high heat. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

3Add the chicken to the pan and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes.

4Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.

5 Add the shrimp to the pan and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from pan and set aside.

6Add the red and green bell peppers and onion to the pan and cook until slightly softened, about 2-3 minutes.

7Add the chicken and shrimp back to the pan, along with the sauce mixture. Cook until the sauce has thickened and everything is well coated, about 1-2 minutes.

8Serve hot with rice.
